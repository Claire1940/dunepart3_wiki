Adapting Frank Herbert's *Dune Messiah* presents unique challenges for Denis Villeneuve and co-writer Brian K. Vaughan. Unlike the action-heavy first novel, *Messiah* is an intellectual, political thriller focused on philosophical dialogues and slow-burning conspiracies. Here is how the production team is structuring the narrative transition.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establishing the Atreides Hegemony">
    The film opens years after Paul's victory. We witness the terrifying scale of the Fremen Jihad, which has claimed billions of lives across the galaxy, showing the dark reality of Paul's rise to power.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Conspiracy Unfolds">
    The Bene Gesserit, the Spacing Guild, and the Tleilaxu form a secret alliance. They introduce Scytale and a mysterious ghola (clone) named Hayt—who carries the face of Paul's fallen mentor, Duncan Idaho (played by Jason Momoa)—to destabilize Paul's psyche.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stilgar's Internal Conflict">
    As the conspiracy closes in, Stilgar must manage internal Fremen unrest. Many traditional Fremen feel the ecological transformation of Arrakis is destroying their culture, forcing Stilgar to choose between his old ways and his new Emperor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Tragic Climax">

  </Step>
