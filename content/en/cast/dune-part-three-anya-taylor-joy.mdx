<Section icon={Target} title="Alia's Story Arc in Dune Messiah">

The narrative of the third film is directly adapted from Frank Herbert's 1969 novel, *Dune Messiah*. This story jumps forward in time, showing the dark consequences of Paul's holy war, which has swept across the known universe.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Imperial High Priestess">
    Alia serves as the head of the religious movement worshiping Paul. She holds massive influence over the Fremen pilgrims but grows increasingly isolated due to her strange powers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Arrival of the Ghola">
    The Bene Tleilax present Paul with a gift: a ghola (clone) of Duncan Idaho named Hayt. Alia is tasked with investigating this resurrected warrior, sparking a deep and complex relationship.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Uncovering the Conspiracy">
    As political enemies like Princess Irulan, the Spacing Guild, and Scytale plot to assassinate Paul, Alia must use her prescient sight and physical combat skills to defend the throne.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Ultimate Sacrifice">
    When the conspiracy reaches its climax, Alia is forced to make critical decisions to preserve the Atreides bloodline, setting up her future role as Regent of the Empire.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Faithful Adaptation">
Director Denis Villeneuve has stated that his goal with the third film is to highlight the cautionary message of Frank Herbert's second book—deconstructing the "hero's journey" and showing the dangers of blind fanaticism.
